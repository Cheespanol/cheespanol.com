1. What Information Do We Collect?

In Short: We collect personal information that you provide to us.

We collect personal information that you voluntarily provide to us when you register on the Services, express an interest in obtaining information about us or our products and Services, when you participate in activities on the Services, or otherwise when you contact us.

Personal Information Provided by You. The personal information we collect may include the following:

  • Email addresses
  • Names
  • Timezone (used for lesson scheduling and cancellation policy enforcement)
  • Display name (shown to teachers and within the platform)

Learning Personalization Data. To deliver a personalized learning experience, we collect information you voluntarily provide about your interests and goals, including: hobbies, profession, family situation, learning goals, preferred lesson pace, previous Spanish experience, vocabulary focus areas, corrections preference, and native language. This information is used by your teacher to customize lesson content, homework assignments, and flashcard topics to your individual needs. You can review and update this information at any time in the app's Settings > Content Personalization section.

Lesson & Progress Data. As part of delivering our educational services, we collect and store: lesson history (dates, topics covered, teacher notes), homework submissions (text, audio recordings, and file uploads), flashcard review progress and mastery data, CEFR level assessments (assigned by your teacher), weekly activity and streak data, and curriculum progress.

Lesson Recordings. Every live lesson conducted through the platform is automatically recorded (video and audio) via Zoom. Recordings are stored securely on Cloudflare R2 and are accessible to you indefinitely through the app's My Lessons section. By using our Services and participating in lessons, you consent to the recording of all lesson sessions. Recordings are used solely for your learning benefit (review and replay) and for quality assurance purposes.

Referral Data. If you participate in our referral program, we collect your referral code, the identities of users you have referred, and referral reward status (credits earned, discounts applied).

Sensitive Information. We do not process sensitive information.

Payment Data. We may collect data necessary to process your payment if you choose to make purchases, such as your payment instrument number, and the security code associated with your payment instrument. All payment data is handled and stored by Paddle and Airwallex. You may find their privacy notice links here: Paddle and Airwallex.

Social Media Login Data. We may provide you with the option to register with us using your existing social media account details, like your Google or Apple account. If you choose to register in this way, we will collect certain profile information about you from the social media provider, as described in the section "How Do We Handle Your Social Logins?" below.

Application Data. If you use our application(s), we also may collect the following information if you choose to provide us with access or permission:

  • Mobile Device Access. We may request access or permission to certain features from your mobile device, including your mobile device's notifications, and other features. If you wish to change our access or permissions, you may do so in your device's settings.
  • Push Notifications. We may request to send you push notifications regarding your account or certain features of the application(s). If you wish to opt out from receiving these types of communications, you may turn them off in your device's settings.

This information is primarily needed to maintain the security and operation of our application(s), for troubleshooting, and for our internal analytics and reporting purposes.

All personal information that you provide to us must be true, complete, and accurate, and you must notify us of any changes to such personal information.

11. Controls for Do-Not-Track Features

Most web browsers and some mobile operating systems include a Do-Not-Track ("DNT") feature or setting you can activate to signal your privacy preference not to have data about your online browsing activities monitored and collected. At this stage, no uniform technology standard for recognizing and implementing DNT signals has been finalized. As such, we do not currently respond to DNT browser signals or any other mechanism that automatically communicates your choice not to be tracked online.
